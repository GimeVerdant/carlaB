How I work

My approach

Therapy is not a fixed method applied to a problem — it is a living encounter. Here is some of what shapes how I work.

The framework

Integrative transpersonal psychotherapy

The privilege of a lifetime is to become who you truly are. — C.G. Jung

My training at Re-Vision College, London forms the bedrock of how I work. Integrative therapy means drawing on a range of psychological traditions — psychodynamic, humanistic, existential — rather than following a single fixed model. I follow the person in front of me, not a prescribed sequence.

Transpersonal means I attend to the deeper dimensions of experience: meaning, spiritual inquiry, the parts of us that reach beyond ordinary identity. Together these create a spacious, responsive way of working. Nothing is forced. Everything is held.

A relationship built on trust

What we need is someone who believes in us, even when we cannot believe in ourselves. Anon

How we work

Everything in therapy happens through the relationship. The quality of connection between us — the consistency, the honesty, the care — is itself therapeutic. I work relationally, paying close attention to what unfolds between us: what is said and what remains unsaid, moments of rupture and repair.

I bring warmth and directness in equal measure. You do not have to perform wellness here, or arrive with the right words. Sessions are fifty minutes, weekly to begin with, adjusted as the work finds its rhythm.

Tell me, what is it you plan to do with your one wild and precious life?

— Mary Oliver, The Summer Day

Drawing, dreaming, making meaning

The methods

Drawing, dreaming, making meaning

Alongside talking, I sometimes invite drawing — not as art-making, but as a way of reaching places words alone cannot. Dream work, too, can open surprising doors. My background in Fine Art means I approach sessions with the same quality of open enquiry I once brought to the studio: patient, curious, without agenda.

Nothing is required except a willingness to explore. If images and dreams feel foreign to you, we simply follow what feels alive. The work shapes itself around you.
