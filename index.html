Your story

Healing begins where the wound was made.

"Healing begins where the wound was made" — Alice Walker

Telling your story can be an important part of therapy — reflecting on your past, your childhood, and the relational and emotional environments that have shaped you. We may also consider intergenerational patterns: the ways experiences, beliefs, and wounds can be carried across families, often outside of conscious awareness.

We can also work with where you are now, focusing on your current experience and where you would like to get to. Therapy can move fluidly between past and present, always guided by what feels most relevant and supportive for you.

The framework

My training at Re-Vision College draws on a range of traditions — psychodynamic, humanistic, existential, transpersonal — creating a spacious, responsive way of working that adapts to who you are. I follow the person in front of me, not a prescribed sequence.

Everything in therapy happens through the relationship. I pay close attention to what unfolds between us — what is said and what remains unsaid, moments of rupture and repair. I bring warmth and directness in equal measure.

The methods

Alongside talking, I sometimes invite drawing or dream work as a way of reaching places words alone cannot. My Fine Art background means I approach sessions with the same patient, curious enquiry I once brought to the studio — without agenda, following what feels alive.
